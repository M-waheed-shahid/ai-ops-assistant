# Marketing Content Guidelines

**Department:** Marketing
**Last Updated:** February 2026
**Owner:** Head of Marketing

## Content Pillars
1. **AI adoption case studies** — anonymized client results (always require client sign-off before publishing)
2. **Educational content** — practical AI/automation guidance for operations leaders at mid-sized companies
3. **Company culture** — hiring, team spotlights, event recaps

## Brand Voice
Direct, practical, non-hype. We avoid buzzword-heavy language ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "cutting-edge") in favor of specific, concrete claims backed by real outcomes. Tone is confident but not salesy.

## Case Study Approval Process
1. Draft written by Marketing, using the Customer Success handoff packet and a short client interview
2. Reviewed by the Engagement Lead for technical accuracy
3. Sent to client for written approval — **no case study is published without explicit client sign-off**, even if anonymized
4. Final review by Head of Marketing before publishing

## Channels
- Company blog (published weekly)
- LinkedIn (primary channel — 3x/week posting cadence)
- Monthly webinar series ("AI Ops Office Hours")
- Email newsletter (bi-weekly, ~2,400 subscribers as of Q1 2026)

## Lead Sourcing from Marketing
All webinar registrants and content downloads are logged as Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs) in Salesforce. MQLs are routed to Sales within 48 hours for initial outreach. Conversion from MQL to Qualified pipeline stage is currently ~12%.

## Review Cadence
Marketing performance (traffic, MQLs, webinar attendance) is reviewed monthly against quarterly targets set jointly with Sales leadership.
